SparkIt Privacy

Privacy Policy

This policy explains how SparkIt may collect, use, retain, and share information connected to this website, project inquiries, lead forms, analytics, and advertising.

Last updated: July 19, 2026

1. Overview

SparkIt Design, referred to in this policy as “SparkIt,” “we,” “us,” or “our,” respects the privacy of website visitors, prospective customers, clients, and people who communicate with us.

This Privacy Policy describes how SparkIt may collect, use, retain, protect, and disclose information when you:

  • Visit SparkItDesign.com
  • Send SparkIt an email
  • Submit a website or advertising lead form
  • Respond to a SparkIt promotion
  • Request pricing or project information
  • Purchase or receive SparkIt services
  • Communicate with SparkIt through social media

By using this website or voluntarily submitting information to SparkIt, you acknowledge the practices described in this policy.

2. Information you provide

SparkIt may collect information you voluntarily provide, including:

  • Your name
  • Business or brokerage name
  • Email address
  • Telephone number
  • Website address
  • Social media profile information
  • Business location or service area
  • Listing or property information
  • Project goals, timelines, and service interests
  • Photos, videos, logos, and other creative materials
  • Messages, questions, feedback, and correspondence
  • Billing or transaction details when required for a project

Please do not submit Social Security numbers, government identification numbers, passwords, health information, financial account credentials, or other highly sensitive information through website forms, lead forms, email, or social media messages.

3. Information collected automatically

When you visit this website, SparkIt and its service providers may automatically collect technical or usage information, including:

  • Pages viewed
  • Approximate visit time and session activity
  • Referring website or campaign source
  • Browser type
  • Device type
  • Operating system
  • Approximate geographic location
  • Interactions with links, forms, or advertisements
  • Cookie, advertising, or similar technology identifiers

This information is generally used to understand website performance, improve content, measure advertising activity, and identify broad patterns in how visitors use the website.

4. How SparkIt uses information

SparkIt may use information to:

  • Respond to questions and project inquiries
  • Provide requested pricing or service information
  • Prepare proposals, estimates, plans, or agreements
  • Create and deliver video, social media, or marketing services
  • Communicate about active projects
  • Provide customer support
  • Process legitimate business transactions
  • Improve SparkIt services and website content
  • Measure marketing and advertising performance
  • Prevent misuse, fraud, or security incidents
  • Maintain appropriate business and tax records
  • Comply with applicable legal obligations

SparkIt may also use inquiry information to follow up about the service, offer, or project you requested. SparkIt does not intend to use submitted information for unrelated purposes without an appropriate reason or permission.

5. Realtor and property materials

Customers may provide listing photos, property details, agent information, brokerage information, logos, and calls to action so SparkIt can create real estate video content.

You are responsible for confirming that you have the necessary rights, permissions, licenses, and approvals to provide and use any photos, property details, trademarks, logos, music, footage, testimonials, or other materials submitted to SparkIt.

Do not provide private residential information that is not intended for public marketing or information that could create a safety or privacy risk for a homeowner, tenant, buyer, seller, or other person.

6. Meta, Facebook, and Instagram leads

SparkIt may use Meta platforms, including Facebook and Instagram, to publish advertisements, receive messages, and collect information through lead forms.

When you submit a Meta lead form, SparkIt may receive the information you choose to provide, such as your name, email address, phone number, business information, service interest, and answers to form questions.

SparkIt uses this information to respond to the promotion, service, or request connected to the form. Meta may separately collect and process information under its own terms and privacy policies.

7. Google Analytics and Google Tag Manager

SparkIt uses Google Analytics and Google Tag Manager to understand website traffic, page activity, referral sources, campaign performance, and general website usage.

Google Analytics may process information such as session statistics, approximate location, device information, browser information, and website interactions. SparkIt does not intend to send names, email addresses, telephone numbers, or other directly identifying contact-form information to Google Analytics.

Google processes information according to its own terms, contracts, technologies, and privacy practices. You may learn more through Google’s privacy materials:

Google Privacy Policy

8. Meta Pixel

SparkIt uses the Meta Pixel to help measure website visits, advertising activity, campaign performance, and actions taken after someone views or interacts with an advertisement.

Meta may use cookies, browser information, device information, website activity, and other identifiers in accordance with its own policies and user settings.

You may learn more through Meta’s privacy materials:

Meta Privacy Policy

9. Cookies and similar technologies

SparkIt and its service providers may use cookies, pixels, tags, local storage, and similar technologies to operate the website, remember technical preferences, measure visits, analyze performance, and support advertising.

Cookies are small data files stored by a browser or device. Some cookies may be necessary for website functionality, while others may support analytics or advertising.

You may be able to block, delete, or limit cookies through your browser or device settings. Disabling cookies may affect certain website features or measurement functions.

10. Advertising choices

You may manage certain advertising preferences through your Facebook, Instagram, Meta, Google, browser, or mobile-device settings.

You may also use browser tools that limit cookies or tracking. These settings are controlled by the relevant browser, platform, or service provider rather than SparkIt.

11. How SparkIt may disclose information

SparkIt does not sell contact-form submissions or project leads for money.

SparkIt may disclose information when reasonably necessary to:

  • Complete a requested project or service
  • Work with hosting, email, analytics, advertising, payment, or project-management providers
  • Work with approved contractors or creative collaborators
  • Protect SparkIt, its customers, or other people
  • Investigate suspected fraud, misuse, or security concerns
  • Comply with a valid legal request or legal obligation
  • Support a merger, sale, restructuring, or transfer of the business

Service providers may process information only as necessary to perform the services they provide, subject to their own agreements and legal responsibilities.

12. Client campaigns and third-party leads

SparkIt may support advertising, content, or lead-generation work for a client. When SparkIt receives information on behalf of a client, the information may also be subject to that client’s privacy practices.

SparkIt will use client campaign information for legitimate campaign, reporting, communication, or service-delivery purposes. SparkIt does not intend to combine one client’s private lead data with another client’s campaign for unrelated marketing.

13. Data retention

SparkIt retains information for as long as reasonably necessary to respond to inquiries, perform services, maintain customer and business records, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, support security, and satisfy legal, accounting, or tax obligations.

Retention periods may vary based on the type of information, the nature of the relationship, the services requested, and applicable business or legal requirements.

SparkIt may delete or anonymize information when it is no longer reasonably needed, subject to legitimate recordkeeping requirements.

14. Data security

SparkIt uses reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures intended to protect information against unauthorized access, loss, misuse, alteration, or disclosure.

No website, email account, form, cloud platform, storage system, or internet transmission can be guaranteed completely secure. You should use care when deciding what information to submit electronically.

15. Your privacy choices and requests

Depending on where you live and applicable law, you may have the right to request:

  • Information about personal information SparkIt maintains
  • Access to personal information you submitted
  • Correction of inaccurate information
  • Deletion of certain information
  • Restriction of certain uses
  • Withdrawal of a previous consent

These rights may be subject to legal exceptions, identity verification, recordkeeping obligations, contractual requirements, and the scope of applicable law.

To submit a privacy request, email sparkitga@gmail.com with the subject line “Privacy Request.”

SparkIt may ask for information reasonably necessary to confirm your identity and locate the relevant records.

16. Children’s privacy

SparkIt’s website and services are intended for businesses and adults. SparkIt does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 or intentionally direct lead-generation advertising to children.

A parent or guardian who believes a child has submitted information may contact SparkIt to request its review and deletion.

17. External websites and platforms

This website may link to social networks, email services, websites, payment systems, or other third-party platforms. SparkIt does not control the privacy, security, content, or data practices of third-party services.

Review the privacy policy of each external service before providing personal information.

18. Changes to this policy

SparkIt may update this Privacy Policy as its services, technologies, vendors, advertising practices, or legal obligations change.

Updates will be posted on this page with a revised “Last updated” date. Material changes may also be communicated through another reasonable method when appropriate.
