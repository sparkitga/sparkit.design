Video Strategy for Local Brands

Three Videos Every Local Brand Should Create First

The best local marketing respects people’s time, solves real problems, and gives busy customers a reason to remember your brand.

Local business owners are busy serving customers, managing schedules, completing certifications, handling paperwork, and keeping revenue moving. Realtors are preparing listings, coordinating showings, answering clients, and working through continuing education requirements. The first video should make that workload feel lighter by creating interest through entertainment. Think sports-inspired Reels, quick wins, local moments, or behind-the-scenes content that shows the personality and people behind the brand.

1. Create Reels That Earn Interest

Use energy, entertainment, local culture, and relatable moments to stop the scroll before asking the viewer to buy.

2. Create Reels That Teach

Explain one useful idea as clearly as you would onboard a new teammate or help a client understand the next step.

3. Create Helpful How-To Videos

Demonstrate a process, product, or decision in a practical style similar to the helpful videos people expect from trusted home-improvement brands.

The second video should teach something useful. Approach it like helping a new teammate learn a skill. A realtor could explain how homeowners should prepare for listing photos, while a contractor could teach customers how to recognize an early problem. This content respects the viewer’s time and helps the brand become a trusted resource instead of another company asking for attention.

The third video should show people how to complete a task or make a better decision. Think practical Home Depot-style product demonstrations, but focused on the customer’s real questions. Show the process, explain the tools, and remove confusion. Start by entertaining people, earn trust by teaching them, and prove your value by helping them move forward.
